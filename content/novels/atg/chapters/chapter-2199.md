Heaven Breaker Divine Kingdom

This vast courtyard belonged solely to the Divine Daughter of Heaven Breaker. The first thing to catch the eye was the sea of colorful cloud flowers, a gift once bestowed by the Lingxian Divine Officer, now fully grown and flourishing.

The sky was serene and clear, the sea of colorful cloud flowers reflecting the sword radiance of the azure heavens, spreading a soft glow like a dreamlike flowing sunset. Petals of the colorful cloud flowers fluttered gently in the breeze, carpeting the ground with pure white fragrant dust. Here, there was no abyssal dust to gnaw at the bones, no biting cold wind, no worldly disturbances—only the sword intent and spiritual breath drifting leisurely. It was the finest scenery in the mortal realm, perfect for the melodies of qin and the dance of swords, companions from dawn to dusk.

Beneath a jade tree, Yun Che sat quietly, his sleeves hanging loosely, his features gentle and refined. A faint red mist of flames enveloped him. He lifted his finger lightly, and the Vermilion Bird Flame transformed into strands of crimson flowing fire, forming an invisible fiery qin. The red flames served as strings, entwined with drifting rosy light, radiating a warm glow that spread in all directions.

Before him stood Hua Caili, positioned at the edge of the sea of colorful cloud flowers. She wore a moon-white long dress, the hem adorned with seven shining stars, slowly turning with sword intent. Now a married woman, her black hair was lightly pinned up, a few strands of blue silk fluttering in the wind. Her brows and eyes were increasingly ethereal and transcendent. In her hand, the Liyun Sword gleamed with clear light, reflecting the sword radiance filling the sky.

The pure white, flawless branches of the colorful cloud flowers were regarded by abyssal creatures as flowers of a celestial paradise. Even the profound cultivators of the Divine Kingdom, upon seeing them in this pure land, would be amazed and enchanted. She stood at the edge of the flower sea, yet the divine radiance of the colorful cloud flowers was subdued, their elegance dimmed, as if a true fairy of a dreamlike world had descended into the mortal realm.

But when she looked at Yun Che, her beautiful eyes were filled with endless, flawless, tender affection... as if she were pouring out her heart to him alone, not a mistaken fall into the mortal world, but bound to him in heart and soul, never willing to part.

Yun Che plucked his fingers lightly, and the first movement of the Vermilion Bird’s Consoling Spirit Melody, “Flame Dawn Ode,” gently spread.

The qin music was clear, harmonious, and warm—like the rising morning sun breaking through the mist. The glowing red flames accompanied the strings, silently swirling and dancing, transforming into countless tiny sparks that hovered around Hua Caili. She lightly tapped the fragrant dust with her toes, her figure dancing gracefully. The jade sword rose lightly, slicing out clear and exquisite sword shadows. The sword intent flowed slowly with the fiery stars, like a bird just awakening and fluttering its wings—light, graceful, and winding. The crimson flowing fire intertwined with the pure white sword blade, sometimes soaring across the sky, sometimes falling softly.

Gradually, the melody slowed, seamlessly transitioning from “Flame Dawn Ode” into “Peaceful Spirit Quarters.” The music became deep, soft, and lingering, like the evening breeze returning to the forest, quietly settling the dust and noise. The Vermilion Bird Flame shifted from light, starry brilliance to a gentle, warm red gauze. Hua Caili’s sword shadows also slowed, spinning and folding the sword, stepping lightly. The sword intent became gentle and smooth like a stream, containing profound peace and tranquility. The sword radiance flowed, and the gauzy red flames twisted gracefully with the sword’s momentum.

One flame, one sword—perfectly fused and harmonized.

At this moment, Hua Caili smiled faintly and glanced back, her eyes flowing directly into Yun Che’s. Their gazes met, and in that instant, it was as if each saw in the other the most dazzling and beautiful scenery of this life and world.

Far above in the sky, Hua Qingying’s clear eyes quietly observed, watching their music and dance, their brows and eyes perfectly in tune. She, who cultivated the ruthless sword path and never touched mortal desires, suddenly understood clearly what it meant for phoenix and dragon to sing in harmony.

Hua Caili’s swordsmanship in this life was all shaped by her. Yet she did not know that her sword posture could be so enchantingly beautiful, and her sword heart was pure and immaculate, filled only with joy.

She did not know how long she had been quietly watching, unmoving, unwilling to leave, as if even time itself had slowed its pace.

Then suddenly, the qin music shifted sharply, a resounding momentum rising. The third movement of the Vermilion Bird’s Consoling Spirit Melody, “Burning Calamity Tune,” broke through the air.

The red flames blazed fiercely, surging as if the shadow of the Vermilion Bird was spreading its wings and crying out in fury. Hua Caili’s eyes sharpened, her sword intent suddenly becoming fierce and sharp. The Liyun Sword emitted a piercing scream that split the sky. The sword momentum was swift and unparalleled, the sword intent and red flames entwined and interwoven, stirring a storm of falling petals across the sky.

Amidst the fiery glow, her flowing sleeves fluttered like drifting clouds. She bore the bearing of a sword immortal breaking calamity, and the grace of a Divine Daughter transcending the world. Every breath and moment was breathtakingly beautiful, her elegance like a dream.

At this moment, Yun Che slowly lifted his gaze, his eyes burning with a crimson light dozens of times deeper than before.

The fiery strings between his fingers blazed brilliantly, spreading the vast melody that instantly filled the entire courtyard, covering the whole sea of colorful cloud flowers.

The music was vast and clear, like flowing clouds crossing the blue sky, like a compassionate ferry guiding through the mortal world. Behind Yun Che, a gigantic Vermilion Bird divine shadow spread its wings, scattering countless red feathers that poured forth immense fiery radiance.

Far above in the sky, Hua Qingying’s eyes trembled intensely. At that moment, a voice came from beside her:

“This is... the Heavenly Feather Compassionate Ferry Music.”

Hua Fuchen, who had appeared here unknown to when, stood beside Hua Qingying. Like her, he had not disturbed the scene before them. When the Vermilion Bird divine shadow appeared, he finally let out a soft sigh of admiration.

“The records say that among the three great world-burning heavenly tomes, the Vermilion Bird’s Consoling Spirit Melody is the hardest to master, because it not only requires the perfect harmony of bloodline and body but also the deepest bond of the soul.”

“The Lingxian Divine Officer’s divine attendant, Su Shang, was the most compatible person found by the Lingxian Divine Officer in this era. From what I know, Su Shang, who was the most attuned to the Vermilion Bird Divine Flame of this age, took nine years to successfully perform the first four movements. But the fifth movement, ‘Heavenly Feather Compassionate Ferry Music,’ took one thousand four hundred years to master.”

“And Yun Che… was granted by the Spirit Immortal Divine Officer atop the Pure Land only a few short months ago.”

As a Divine Sovereign, standing at the highest pinnacle of the Profound Dao in this era, he naturally understood better than anyone the terrifying and world-shaking blaze currently burning within Yun Che.

Hua Qingying did not glance sideways, speaking calmly, “To others, it is indeed earth-shattering and epoch-making. But to Yun Che, it is almost ‘ordinary.’”

Such an outrageous assessment did not surprise Hua Fuchen. He had personally witnessed Yun Che, at the Divine Lord realm, defeat someone at the Divine Annihilation realm. He had seen the ancient and solemn face of the Great Divine Official bloom with astonishment because of Yun Che… After a moment, he smiled faintly and sighed deeply, “His future achievements are immeasurable, unimaginable. To speak without much respect, the ultimate realm Yun Che will reach may even surpass that of the Abyss Emperor.”

“It’s laughable that I once raged and panicked over Cai Li’s union with him. It turns out, he is not only truly Cai Li’s destined one but also a fortune never before seen in our Heaven Breaker lineage.”

Hua Qingying said, “For Cai Li, it is also a blessing. Haven’t you noticed that in the month since she married Yun Che, she only occasionally practices swordsmanship, and even then, it is more like dancing with the sword, playfully harmonizing with her husband. Yet the sword intent she naturally releases while wielding the sword rapidly approaches the ‘Milky Way Flow’ realm… Truly remarkable.”

“Sword heart luminous…”

Hua Fuchen softly murmured these four words—the simplest yet most difficult pinnacle of the sword path—the luminous sword heart. Hua Qingying cultivated the ruthless sword path, pursuing a sword heart luminous forged from ruthlessness and desirelessness. But Hua Cai Li, with the most passionate and flawless affection, was truly approaching a luminous sword heart even Hua Qingying had yet to perfect.

“So…” Hua Qingying’s jade lips moved slightly, emitting a faintly confused whisper, “Was my choice wrong from the very beginning…?”

Endless crimson feathers scattered like a vast red glow, layer upon layer rolling out, dyeing half the sky red and engulfing the sword formation overhead. Hua Cai Li rose into the air, her vast sword intent soaring upward, sword shadows weaving densely, merging seamlessly with the flowing crimson clouds.

The glow of the clouds circled the sword, the sword chasing the crimson feathers. The young girl’s eyes and brows held a smile; every rise and fall was like an exiled immortal descending to the mortal world—lively and exquisitely beautiful, revealing the radiant elegance never before seen by others.

The sword wind stirred the warmth of red flames, lifting countless colorful cloud petals to dance wildly, decorating the chase and intertwining of the jade sword and crimson feathers. Hua Cai Li’s sword momentum suddenly slowed… Petals fluttered like butterflies, crimson feathers danced gently through the air, the white and red trajectories in her eyes sometimes slow, sometimes swift, sometimes turning, sometimes overlapping, tracing the most free and chaotic yet absolutely beautiful and soul-cleansing paths.

This scene gradually entered her soul sea from her beautiful eyes, silently imprinting into her sword heart… Thousands of trajectories reflected thousands of sword scars within her sword heart.

She turned her figure, the jade sword poised in the air, softly chanting, “Husband, receive my sword.”

Beneath the flowing crimson clouds, she descended swiftly, sword shadow pressing directly toward Yun Che.

Yun Che smiled faintly, a solitary cloud appearing as a domineering and peerless sword aura instantly erupted, charging forward.

The two swords neared, and in an instant, Yun Che’s overwhelming sword aura was shattered into thousands of fragments of varying sizes, dispersing before it could fully erupt… Moreover, when it shattered, there was no sound of breaking the profound, nor any sword aura shriek.

Yet Hua Cai Li’s sword momentum did not weaken; instead, a nine-foot sword aura suddenly appeared, slowly approaching within Yun Che’s pupils.

Hua Fuchen and Hua Qingying’s eyes both trembled sharply: “This… is…”

This strange change truly caught Yun Che off guard. His upper body leaned back slightly, instinctively forming a defense. But when the sword aura neared to half a foot, it stopped steadily, emitting a brief faint tremor before instantly dissipating with a light twist of Hua Cai Li’s snow-white hand, spreading a vast yet blade-less sword intent that did not even sever a single colorful cloud petal around, slowly vanishing without a trace.

“Husband, I… finally did it!”

She cried out joyfully, then laughingly pounced onto Yun Che, her jade legs wrapping around his waist without any trace of divine grace, hanging on him with laughter and swaying, unwilling to get down for a long time.

Hua Fuchen’s eyes were stunned, forgetting even to lightly scold the dignified Divine Daughter of Heaven Breaker for such improper behavior.

He murmured softly, “At Eden Cloud Summit, Cai Li was barely touching the sword intent of the Fourth Heaven Breaker Sword, and forcing it always brought backlash. Now, in just four short months, she is… nearly perfect?”

Hua Qingying whispered, “As long as Yun Che is by her side, her sword comprehension will advance by leaps and bounds. So, what exactly is the true ‘sword heart luminous’…”

“But no matter what, Cai Li’s future sword achievements will surpass mine. In this life, with Yun Che accompanying her and Heaven Breaker supporting her, her sword attainments will exceed both you and me. Thus, I seem to have no more worries.”

Hua Fuchen nodded lightly, then suddenly thought of something and abruptly turned his head, “So, could it be that you want to…”

Hua Qingying gently shook her head, “If so, then it is good.”

Her clear eyes gazed at Cai Li, then inadvertently fell upon Yun Che.

She had witnessed Qu Wanxin’s heartbreaking demise, and knew all too well that although Hua Fuchen was a Divine Sovereign, his life was one of solitary hardship. She herself had fallen deep into self-blame and could not—or would not—break free. Therefore, the lingering thought of her remaining life was entrusted to the sword path and the body of Hua Caili. She feared most that she would repeat the old sorrows, feared she would sink again into the bitterness of the mortal world.

Now…

The wind brushed the colorful clouds, fallen petals lightly swirling. A faint trace of a smile, delicate and beautiful, quietly brushed past Hua Qingying’s lips.

Such harmony between phoenix and dragon, deep affection paired in twos, was the finest gift of time, and the most perfect return for all past regrets.

True love does not betray; year after year, they rely on each other. No matter how many storms lie ahead, as long as they stand together, hearts connected, they can overcome all hardships of the world.

The lingering thought of a lifetime was finally fulfilled.

“Husband, I have finally cultivated the Fourth Heaven Breaker Sword. Indeed, as long as you are by my side, everything I do becomes especially simple and effortless.”

“Oh, I haven’t named these four swords yet. Auntie and the Seven Sword Sovereigns mentioned this before, but I only want to name them together with you.”

The Heaven Breaker Sword was not a mere sword technique, but a sword intent. Each layer of sword intent could easily integrate into the sword moves she had practiced all her life, or flow freely with her will, spawning endless variations.

She pressed close to Yun Che’s body, whispering softly, “Father God’s First Heaven Breaker Sword is named ‘Soul of the Painting,’ the Second is ‘Eyes of the Painting,’ the Third is ‘Bones of the Painting’… When I was young, I didn’t understand why Father God gave such strange names to his Heaven Breaker Swords. It wasn’t until I met you, Husband, that I truly understood—Father God’s sword heart was filled with the image of Mother. Every sword he wielded depicted Mother’s form within his sword heart.”

Even his divine name, “Painting Heart,” bore the inscription of “Qu Wanxin.”

“Auntie’s sword names sound much more beautiful. The First Sword was originally called ‘Cold Shadow Severing the Azure Dust,’ but was changed to ‘Absolute Shadow Reflecting the Life of Li’ for me. The Second Sword is ‘Shadow Deep Unknown Place,’ the Third ‘Moonfall Pulling the Distant Shadow,’ and the Fourth ‘Lonely Shadow Resting in Jade Light’…”

Yun Che smiled and said, “Auntie’s Heaven Breaker Sword names are ethereal and elegant, fitting her title as Sword Immortal perfectly. Yet each name carries a hint of loneliness and coldness. Auntie…”

He lowered his voice, leaning close to the girl’s ear, “Could it be that she really intends to live alone for her entire life?”

Hua Caili pursed her lips slightly, smiling softly, “If it were before, probably yes.”

“Hmm? Before?”

A glimmer of hope flickered in Hua Caili’s eyes as she deliberately lowered her voice: “Although Auntie has never done anything wrong, she has always harbored guilt towards Mother and me. Since I was born, she has stayed by my side to protect me. But now, I have a husband, the best home for this life. Auntie can finally shift her heart away from me and no longer worry so much.”

“I hope even more that one day, Auntie can find, just as I found my husband, someone most suitable for her—someone who can admire her, lean on her. I believe this is Father God and Mother’s wish as well.”

Though she spoke softly, she was certain Auntie could hear her.

Yun Che pondered for a moment: “I think, given Auntie’s nature, that would be very difficult.”

“Yes, very difficult,” Hua Caili agreed deeply. “Back then, Auntie chose the ruthless sword path that greatly diminished the seven emotions and six desires to pursue the ultimate sword heart clarity. She forged the purest and most pristine sword heart. And I was the only aberrant thought she refused to give up.”

“I once secretly asked Father God if Auntie truly intended to live alone forever. Father God said that with Auntie’s temperament and pursuit, she was destined to be pure and solitary in this life. Unless… a man could surpass her in sword path, conquer her, and carve an eternal shadow upon her sword heart.”

But obviously, that was impossible.

Hua Qingying was recognized as the pinnacle of contemporary sword cultivation, a Sword Immortal known throughout the Abyss. In her youth, her extraordinary sword cultivation was unmatched among peers. After reaching the Divine Extreme Realm, she dominated and suppressed all sword cultivators in the world.

Any three of the Seven Heaven Breaker Sword Sovereigns combined could contend with the First Knight Dugu Zhuyuan, whose strength was close to Hua Qingying’s, but even all seven together were no match for her.

Hua Fuchen was even less comparable. Though his True God power towered over the Abyss, in sword cultivation, he was destined to fall far behind.

From afar, Hua Qingying glanced faintly at Hua Fuchen: “Too much talk.” With that, her figure blurred into a blue shadow and vanished from where she stood, instantly appearing beside Hua Caili and Yun Che.

“Auntie!”

Hua Caili lifted her eyes from Yun Che’s neck and called out joyfully, without surprise, and did not leap down from Yun Che.

Hua Qingying reached out, her jade-like fingers nearly touching Hua Caili, but then hesitated with a trace of helplessness, unwilling to forcibly take her away. “Caili, come with me to the sword array. You just perfectly unleashed the Fourth Heaven Breaker Sword for the first time. You need to calm your mind immediately to comprehend and solidify the sword intent.”

Hua Caili was in no hurry at all. Instead, she rested her delicate head back against Yun Che’s neck: “Can it be later? I want to laze on my husband for a while first.”

Yun Che quickly said, “Caili, we should listen to Auntie. I’ll accompany you to the sword array.”

When her husband spoke, the girl never had the strength to refuse: “Then… Husband, carry me there.”

“...Alright. If you’re not afraid of being seen like this…”

“Hehe, I’m not afraid. I want everyone in the world to see.”

“...”

Such a lack of ceremony, such disregard for others… Hua Qingying had long since gotten used to it over the past month, while Hua Fuchen in the distance raised a hand to his forehead, unable to bear watching.
