Xiaodie Divine Kingdom

In the abyssal depths of the palace, layers upon layers of dark profound arrays swept through the thick, dense dark profound energy, extinguishing the already dim light and rendering the entire hall’s atmosphere even more oppressive.

“Jiyao, why did you return alone? Could it be that even you failed to find him?”
God Sovereign Qiheng stood on the high platform in the hall, his hands folded behind his back. His jet-black robes hung like a curtain of night, and from beginning to end he did not turn around, leaving behind only a solitary, cold silhouette. His voice sounded calm and unruffled, yet the faint tremor of dark profound energy around him betrayed that his heart was far from peaceful.

Pan Jiyao bowed his head and said, “Reporting to Your Excellency, I found Pan Buwang, but he… refuses to return.”
Pan Yusheng’s voice remained even, betraying no emotion: “When did you find him?”
“Twenty days ago.”
“For these twenty days, I have followed him closely, always mindful of Your Excellency’s instructions. I have never used force, but instead tried various methods to patiently guide and admonish him, yet…”
Pan Jiyao lowered his head further, unwilling to let others see the complex darkness swelling in his eyes. “His attitude has never changed. I had no choice but to retreat for now.”

As Pan BuwangPan Buwang’s Dao Master, Pan Jiyao should have been one of the people who understood him best in the world. But during these twenty days of close following, the Pan Buwang in his eyes was utterly unfamiliar. Not only had his dark talent undergone a complete transformation, but his temperament, speech, and behavior had changed so drastically it was as if he had become a completely different person, leaving Pan Jiyao feeling deeply estranged.

Pan Yusheng’s voice grew colder by several degrees. “Hmph, only twenty days, and your patience has already withered to this extent? Could it be that, as his former master, you cannot bear his current insults?”
At these words, Pan Jiyao let out a soft sigh that reached Pan Yusheng’s ears. “I would rather he insult and scold me, venting the grievances in his heart. But… since we met, he has never shown me the slightest anger or resentment—only politeness and distance.”

A faint trace of sorrow spilled out with his words, quickly reined in… Pan Jiyao still bore deep guilt toward Pan BuwangPan Buwang, and beneath his cold indifference without hatred or complaint, this guilt was like a piercing poison.

“And this time I have returned because Pan BuwangPan BuwangPan Buwang has something for Your Excellency.”
Pan Yusheng finally moved, frowning as he turned around. “What is it?”
Pan Jiyao raised his hand, and a faint dark profound light drifted upward. The dark light overflowed with Pan Buwang’s dark aura, dissipating upon contact, proving that no one else had ever glimpsed what it concealed.

Pan Yusheng snatched it up, dispersing the dark light to reveal a fairly smooth Mist Sea Abyss Stone. On it, dense characters were inscribed with dark profound power, along with hundreds of seemingly chaotic but actually deeply profound dark light traces.

“Pan… Abyss… Sever… Night…”

At the whispered words, God Sovereign Qiheng’s pupils dilated slightly. He suddenly looked up sharply at Pan Jiyao. “Pan Buwang… what conditions did he put forward!?”
Pan Jiyao slowly shook his head. “He only asks… that no one search for him anymore.”

“…” God Sovereign Qiheng was struck dumb for a long moment.

Anyone who had experienced the Gathering of the Pure Land would know very clearly that even if not to comply with the Abyss Emperor’s will, the Xiaodie Divine Kingdom would have spared no effort to bring Pan Buwang home.

That technique he “self-created” in the Mist Sea, the “Pan Abyss Sever Night,” which had instantly defeated the Star Moon Divine Son atop Eden’s Cloud Peak and amazed the world—how could the Xiaodie Divine Kingdom allow it to fall into foreign hands?

At first, Pan Yusheng had thought Pan Buwang would use it as a bargaining chip to actively seek to return to Xiaodie Divine Kingdom, and he would respond magnanimously.

Not only God Sovereign Qiheng, but all within the Xiaodie Divine Kingdom thought so.

But Pan BuwangPan Buwang’s subsequent distancing, coldness, and rejection were, in their eyes, clearly a show of arrogance relying on “Pan Abyss Sever Night” as leverage—openly refusing but secretly coercing the kingdom to offer greater sincerity and promises to bring him back.

Yet now, this supposed bargaining chip was handed directly into his hands. And the sole request was…

Do not search for me anymore.

At this moment, Pan Yusheng truly understood that Pan Buwang had never been putting on an act—he really did not want to return.

Once, it was the Xiaodie Divine Kingdom that abandoned him. Now, it was he who abandoned the Xiaodie Divine Kingdom without a trace of attachment.

“Your Excellency,” Pan Jiyao tentatively asked, “Should I continue to persuade him?”
Pan Yusheng raised a hand, his voice tinged with a faint sense of powerlessness. “No need. Withdraw.”

Pan Jiyao rose and took two steps back, then stopped and asked, “Your Excellency, although Pan Buwang has handed over the ‘Pan Abyss Sever Night,’ the Abyss Emperor’s words that day were like a heavenly decree. No matter what, we should still…”
“Withdraw!”

Pan Jiyao said no more, bowed, and left.

But clearly, his state of mind had changed greatly from the beginning.

Pan Yusheng clenched the Abyss Stone tightly but did not immediately delve into the profound mysteries of the “Pan Abyss Sever Night.” Instead, he stood there, lost in thought for a long time.

Though his wish was fulfilled, his heart was not filled with joy but with a long-lasting oppression that would not dissipate.

Like Pan Jiyao, he would have preferred Pan Buwang to respond with anger, resentment, insults, or even arrogant demands backed by “Pan Abyss Sever Night” and the Abyss Emperor’s grace.

But why was it instead…

The entire hall was wrapped in a heavy, oppressive silence, as if even the air currents were congealed by the profound darkness.

After a long while, he let out a long, deep sigh facing the gloom ahead.

Heaven Breaker Divine Kingdom, Eight Extremes Sword Courtyard.
This space was forged by eight layers of sword formations, each suffused with eight distinct and overwhelming sword intents. Only the Sword Sovereign’s personally instructed disciples or the core imperial sons were qualified to enter here for comprehension and cultivation.

However, if the Divine Daughter of Heaven Breaker stepped inside, all other disciples must withdraw completely and keep their distance.

“Very good. Such progress is truly rapid. But the Fourth Heaven Breaker Sword requires a massive consumption of profound energy. While refining the sword, you must not neglect your cultivation of the Profound Dao,” said Hua Qingying.

Hua Qingying held the Peerless Immortal Sword firmly. The sword’s blade shimmered with a clear and cold radiance, perfectly matching her solitary and transcendent immortal bearing. These days, she had personally been sparring sword techniques with Hua Caili. Every move, every breath, was imbued with the essence of a Sword Immortal.

“Understood, Auntie,” the young girl replied.

Sweat glistened on her fragrant skin, some drops even falling onto the seven-star light patterns embroidered on her skirt. The white wrist holding the Liyun Sword trembled slightly, nearing exhaustion. Yet when she lifted her gaze, her snowy face was bright with a radiant smile, her eyes clear and flowing like moonlight, showing no trace of weariness or boredom.

This was because not far away, on the jade steps etched with sword patterns, Yun Che stood silently, watching her intently, always by her side.

“Auntie, I want to try the Fifth Heaven Breaker Sword,” Hua Caili said.

Hua Qingying’s brows knitted slightly. “You have already mastered the Fourth Heaven Breaker Sword with ease, but you have yet to reach the Great Perfection realm. To directly comprehend the Fifth Sword is somewhat too hasty. However…”

“This kind of step-by-step rule only applies to sword path mediocrities like your father god. If it’s Caili, then perhaps it’s worth a try.”

From afar, Yun Che secretly gasped. The venerable Sword Sovereign of the Heaven Breaker Divine Kingdom was, in Hua Qingying’s mouth, actually called a “sword path mediocrity”…

“The sword intent of the Fourth Heaven Breaker Sword condenses into ‘Sword Energy’,” Hua Qingying explained, “while the Fifth Heaven Breaker Sword’s intent is called ‘Sword Flow’.”

“Sword… Flow…?”

Hua Caili had long memorized the Heaven Breaker sword manual by heart, but the term “Sword Flow” was something she was hearing for the first time.

She pondered briefly, then slowly raised the Liyun Sword in her hand. The jade-white blade swept through the air, leaving behind layers of lingering sword radiance, like a slowly falling stream of water.

“Is it about extending the sword intent like a flowing stream of water?”

“Yes and no.”

Hua Qingying sheathed the Peerless Immortal Sword and held a single finger horizontally before her. “To master the Fifth Heaven Breaker Sword, you must first comprehend three phrases.”

“Sword is water.”

“Sword is not water.”

“Water is also sword!”

As she finished speaking the nine characters, she softly chanted, “Yun Che, condense water!”

Yun Che immediately obeyed. His palm grasped at empty air, and a stream of water a zhang long and a chi wide condensed beside Hua Qingying, reflecting shimmering light under the myriad sword glimmers.

Hua Qingying’s jade fingers lightly twirled and took only a single drop. Then, using her finger as a sword to transform intent, she pointed this drop of water toward Hua Caili.

“Caili, receive the sword!”

Shhh—

A single drop of ordinary water emitted a piercing sword whistle.

At that moment, Yun Che’s gaze sharpened intensely, his pupils flashing with profound astonishment.

His spiritual senses were far beyond his realm in depth and sensitivity. He was shocked to discover that this water drop, which emitted the sound of breaking through the void, contained no profound energy at all—it was driven purely by sword intent.

What moved his soul even more was that this water drop remained perfectly flawless and round like a full moon from beginning to end. Not only did it not disperse, it did not even deform slightly.

Given the softness and fragility of a water drop, it would naturally deform in flight without external force. Yet under Hua Qingying’s sword intent, it maintained a perfect form as it sliced through the air!

With Yun Che’s current understanding, he could not imagine what level of sword cultivation was required to control sword intent to such an unfathomable degree.

No wonder the Abyss World said that if, in the past, the Heaven Breaker Divine Kingdom had been carried not by Hua Fuchen but by Hua Qingying, then the title of the number one Divine Sovereign of the Six Divine Kingdoms would have surely changed hands.

If she truly achieved true godhood with such sword intent, her power would be unimaginable.

Hua Caili held her breath and focused her mind, lightly meeting the sword. The water drop touched the sword and instantly shattered into countless tiny water mists.

But in the next moment, these scattered mists suddenly reversed and fell, carrying layers upon layers of faint sword intent onto Hua Caili’s body, leaving a momentary dampness on her cheek.

Hua Caili was briefly stunned.

Hua Qingying’s cold immortal voice sounded in her ear: “Profound energy can be resisted, rebounded, and shattered. But sword intent can flow endlessly like a never-ending stream of water—it can circulate when defended against, turn into rain when broken, and condense into mist when dispersed.”

“As long as the sword heart remains, the sword intent will be eternal and undying!”

Hua Caili slowly closed her eyes. After a few breaths, she opened them again and whispered, “Auntie, two months ago, you said your sword path had reached the realm where your mind itself is the sword, and you can sever others’ thoughts with your sword. Is the meaning of ‘Sword Flow’ the embryonic form of that realm?”

Hua Qingying’s brow twitched slightly—a faint sigh and pleasure radiated from her soul and face. She gave no direct answer, nor any movement or fluctuation of profound energy. The water flow around her instantly transformed into ten thousand slender water swords, their cold light sparkling as they hovered in midair.

“The Fifth Heaven Breaker Sword is the most difficult sword domain to cross in the Heaven Breaker sword manual. Your father god and the current Seven Sword Sovereigns all took longer to master the Fifth Sword than the first four combined. But for you, perhaps… it will be even smoother than the Fourth Sword.”

As her voice fell, the ten thousand water swords flew toward Hua Caili and then quietly floated around her in all directions.

“Merge your sword intent into these ten thousand water traces. Not a single one can be missed. Then command the water swords to attack me. After one breath, withdraw the sword intent; after another breath, condense the sword intent again. Repeat this cycle… When you can instantly transform ten thousand flows into swords without losing a single bit during my sword disturbance, you will have a preliminary understanding of the meaning of ‘Sword Flow’!”

Amidst the surging sword qi, every single water trace shifted in position and form with each passing moment—let alone the myriad of them.

Hua Caili pondered briefly. The Liyun Sword suddenly gleamed with a jade-like radiance. Following the sword’s tip, the myriad water traces instantly transformed into swords, each taking a different form as they shot directly at Hua Qingying.

Though unable to reach the terrifying realm of Hua Qingying, this initial strike already revealed the emergence of profound artistic conception, rivaling the insights others might gain over years, fully displaying the demeanor of a young sword immortal.

A flicker of unusual color flashed in Hua Qingying’s eyes. Her figure remained still, only her hair fluttered slightly. In an instant, the trajectories of the myriad water traces reversed, curling back toward Hua Caili.

On the jade steps, Yun Che watched quietly. Hua Qingying resembled a divine figure reflecting the cold moon in the void, Hua Caili’s constantly fluttering ethereal immortal shadow, and the ever-changing sword shadows and traces between the two women—all were captured in perfect detail within his pupils.

In recent days, every word of Hua Qingying’s guidance, imbued with the utmost sword essence, had entered his ears. Yet her vast, boundless, intangible, and silent sword intent was something he sensed even more clearly than Hua Caili’s.

Sword is water... sword is not water... water is sword...

Mind as sword... sword severs mind...

As long as the sword heart remains, the sword intent will eternally endure...

His eyes reflected the two women’s magnificent, dreamlike, and dazzling immortal sword shadows, but within his soul echoed Hua Qingying’s voice again and again. Those simple sword dao maxims, which at first brought him a few points of understanding, but as he unconsciously pondered them repeatedly in his soul, the initial slight enlightenment not only did not become more thorough and clear, but gradually became blurred.

In the end, they dissolved into complete confusion.

Ding...

A soft chime, like the sound of water colliding with sword.

A single droplet of water was shattered from the slightly disordered sword intent, breaking free from the water-shaped sword formation, flying straight toward Yun Che’s position, aiming directly at his eyes.

The water droplet moved extremely fast. As it rapidly approached, the originally tiny water shadow quickly expanded in his vision—larger and larger—until just before touching his pupils, it filled his entire field of view, engulfing the whole world before his eyes.

The originally vast and magnificent sword formation space, dazzling with sword radiance, was transformed into a shimmering orb of water light.

At that moment, all sounds in the world vanished. The entire soul sea plunged into utter silence, as if time itself had suddenly stopped.

A single droplet of water can engulf a world.

A world can be contained within a single droplet of water.

Sword is water, sword is not water, water is sword...

In the strange silence, Yun Che’s arm slowly rose, his fingers forming the shape of a sword.

Law flees into silence...

Chaos becomes heart...

Taihao harmonizes breath...

All... return... to... Tai... Xu...

The faint thought within his soul seemed both his own voice and one from the distant ancient void.

At that moment, his finger lightly flicked out.

In an instant, the sword radiance throughout the entire sword formation space completely froze, as if sealed by an invisible force.

Also frozen was the ever-flowing sword intent of Hua Caili. She stood there stunned, the Liyun Sword in her hand trembling suddenly, emitting sharp sounds like cries of fear.

Hua Qingying abruptly turned her head toward Yun Che’s direction. Then, her cold eyes suddenly contracted to their utmost.

She saw a thread of white light extending from between Yun Che’s fingers. This white light slowly spread within her pupils, cutting through the space of the sword formation, severing the world within her sight, even piercing through her vision to her soul sea, breaking her flawless sword intent at its extreme realm, and directly entering her ancient, pure sword heart...

Hum—

A soft chime. The sword formation trembled as a strange sound, unheard even by Hua Qingying before, resounded. At the same time, Yun Che’s eyes lost color, blood burst from a hundred wounds across his body, and he fell backward stiffly.

“Husband!!!”

Hua Caili’s snowy face paled in horror. The Liyun Sword slipped from her hand as she darted down in panic, tightly embracing Yun Che’s upper body as he nearly crashed to the ground.

At the moment of contact, Hua Caili’s eyes nearly shattered. Because on Yun Che’s body, she could barely sense any trace of profound energy—his energy was so depleted it was less than that of a mortal barely entering the profound realm.

“Husband! Husband, what... what’s wrong with you?” Hua Caili’s voice trembled uncontrollably, tears streaming down her face in sudden terror. “Auntie... please... come quickly and see him, he... he...”

It was only at this moment that Hua Qingying snapped out of her daze.

But the trembling within her soul sea and sword heart showed no sign of ceasing.

She instantly appeared beside Yun Che, touching his forehead with her finger, then softly reassured, “Rest assured, he was only drained of all his profound energy in an instant. The bodily injuries are a backlash from the sword’s might, but fortunately, the backlash isn’t too severe. With his physique and bright profound energy, he only needs a short rest. Once his profound energy recovers somewhat, he will be unharmed.”

As she spoke, gentle strands of profound breath flowed into Yun Che’s meridians and body, visibly easing his previously pale complexion.

Hua Qingying’s words dispelled the fear in Hua Caili’s heart, though tears still stained her face. “That’s good... that’s good...”

At this moment, her entire heart and gaze were fixed on Yun Che’s safety, without a single thought toward the cause or the terrifying anomaly just now. She only held the man in her arms tighter.

Hua Qingying raised her eyes, staring blankly at the white streak still lingering in the sky.

She lifted her hand, her jade-like finger gently touching it. As her fingertip neared, the naturally flowing invisible sword intent slowly faded... more precisely, it was silently stripped away and devoured by an invisible force. Upon contact, a sharp pain was felt.

She withdrew her finger, and on its tip was a slowly oozing drop of blood.

She flipped her palm, and the Absolute Immortal Sword appeared in her palm. But this time, it did not emit the vast sword might that silenced all living beings as before. Instead, it trembled like the Liyun Sword.

One breath... two breaths... three breaths...

The white streak finally faded away and vanished, and the trembling of the Absolute Immortal Sword ceased accordingly.

Hua Qingying sheathed the Absolute Immortal Sword, her gaze falling on the unconscious Yun Che’s body. Her heart churned with unprecedented turmoil since she had stepped onto the path of the sword.
